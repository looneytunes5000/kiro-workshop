# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Beginners to AI coding - developers new to AI-assisted development who need guided, step-by-step introduction to Trae Solo. They are learning independently or at workshops/hackathons, and may feel uncertain about AI-powered development concepts.

## Product Purpose

Teach beginners how to leverage Trae Solo for AI-driven development through a self-paced, accessible workshop experience. Success is measured by users completing the workshop, building confidence in AI coding tools, and feeling empowered to apply these skills to their own projects.

## Brand Personality

Calm, clear, confidence-building. The site should feel like a patient mentor - generous whitespace, measured pacing, minimal noise. Users should feel steady and focused as they progress through lessons, not rushed or overwhelmed. The visual tone says "take your time, everything is clear" while subtly reinforcing mastery and competence.

## Anti-references

- Generic "modern" SaaS dashboards with blue gradients and stock illustrations
- Overly colorful or playful consumer app patterns
- Dense documentation walls with no visual breathing room
- Heavy animations, bouncing elements, or attention-grabbing effects

## Design Principles

1. **Calm over clever** - Prioritize readability and comprehension over novel layouts. Users are learning; don't make them work for it.
2. **Show, don't tell** - Let code examples, screenshots, and concrete demonstrations carry the weight. Minimize explanatory text where examples exist.
3. **Progressive disclosure** - Reveal complexity gradually. Start with the simplest working example, then expand. Use collapsible sections for optional depth.
4. **Visual rest areas** - Generous whitespace, consistent rhythm, and muted backgrounds give users cognitive space to absorb content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA for all interactive elements and text contrast
- Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; non-essential animations should be safe to ignore
- Color blindness: do not rely on color alone to convey meaning. Use labels, icons, or patterns alongside color.
